\begin{abstract}
%==============================================================================
Hallucination \emph{detection} answers a predictive question --- is this output
false? --- but a localization is only causally meaningful if intervening on it
\emph{does something}. We argue that the right bar is not ``can we measure an
above-pool causal contrast honestly'' (an infrastructure claim) but ``can the
localization \emph{license a reusable repair} that a detector cannot.'' We
operationalize this as \textbf{repair-transfer certification}: a trace segment is
certified causally useful for a hallucination class when a training-free,
source-derived localized repair \emph{policy}, applied to held-out in-class
\emph{target} examples, recovers factuality beyond a matched-null repair, at a
bounded edit budget and a bounded utility cost. The certification statistic
$\Rhat$ (cross-example, repair-style) is the headline; the within-example
necessity statistic $\Uhat$ --- a matched-null Causal Intervention-Usefulness
(CIU) estimand with a per-example null pool matched on operator, budget, length,
layers, reference, and example --- is demoted to a \emph{screening} statistic.
We name the source$\to$target transport map and the five assumptions (A5--A9:
SUTVA-for-repair, within-class exchangeability, positivity, reference validity,
matched-null repair validity) under which $\Rhat$ is an identified causal
estimand, and prove an \textbf{identification lemma} (Lemma~\ref{lem:rident})
that splits the assumption-free part (a definitional matched-null subtraction)
from the assumption-loaded causal part, mirroring the CIU tautology/assumption
split. Because example indices recur across many pairs, $\Rhat$ is treated as a
within-class \textbf{U-statistic} with a confirmatory \textbf{two-way
(source,target) cluster bootstrap} CI, corroborated by a \textbf{class-block
sign-flip diagnostic}, not a paired bootstrap. Every data-adaptive choice --- the proximity binning, the repair
policy, the class weights --- is a \textbf{selection event} paid for by a
selection split or a Bonferroni penalty over an enumerated grid. The crucial
contrast is made \emph{empirical}, not rhetorical: a baseline panel B0--B5 feeds
detector-selected localizations (TraceDet-, entropy-, probe-selected) through the
\emph{identical} repair pipeline, and our novelty is \emph{conditional} on
out-transferring them. An adversarial oracle (Axis X$'$) includes a
\emph{provable} control-failure regime: a confounder collinear with the leakage
controls that they cannot see, where the registered, falsifiable prediction is
that $\Rhat$ itself collapses --- and if it does not, the paper reports the
method unsound for that confounder class. The lead paradigm is autoregressive
LLMs; reasoning and diffusion are a transfer study, with TraceDet additionally
entering as a repair-baseline. This paper states the method, discharges the
theory, and pre-registers the full analysis plan and kill-gates. \textbf{No
empirical result is reported}: every table and figure is a
\textsc{data\_needed} placeholder, and \emph{empirical validation follows the
pre-registered, authorized run} (\texttt{server.authorized} remains
\texttt{false}). The contribution is the certified estimand and its
identification design.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%==============================================================================

\paragraph{The gap: detection $\ne$ causal usefulness.} Large language models
hallucinate, and a large literature now \emph{detects} hallucinations from output
uncertainty, sampling consistency, or internal states
\cite{farquhar2024semantic,manakul2023selfcheckgpt,chen2024inside,ji2023survey}.
Detection answers a predictive question --- \emph{is this output likely false?}
The closest neighbours to this work operate on \emph{traces} but still ask a
detection question: \textbf{TraceDet} \cite{tracedet2026diffusion} extracts the
information-bottleneck sub-trace of a diffusion decoding trace and reports a
concrete downstream win ($+15.2\%$ AUROC average); fine-grained causal tracing in
vision-language models (\textbf{FCCT}/\textbf{IRI}) \cite{li2026fcct} quantifies a
fixed-grid \emph{recovery rate} of components under Gaussian perturbation. Neither
asks whether a \emph{localization} is causally useful in the operational sense
that matters for auditing: \emph{does editing it license something a detector
cannot}? A segment can be highly predictive of hallucination yet causally inert (a
symptom), and causally decisive yet carry no salient detector signal. The two
questions come apart precisely where intervention matters.

\paragraph{Why ``honest measurement'' is not enough.} A natural first answer ---
and the answer of the matched-null CIU estimand we build on (Sec.~\ref{sec:ciu})
--- is to \emph{measure} an above-pool causal contrast honestly: edit the selected
segment, edit a budget/length/layer/reference/example-matched random segment, and
report the difference. That is leakage-safe and reproducible, but it certifies the
claim by \emph{internal consistency}: the estimator is unbiased for a contrast the
method itself defines, and every internal control checks that the \emph{estimator}
is well-behaved --- never that the localized segment is \emph{useful for anything
a detector cannot already do}. Honest measurement of a self-defined quantity reads
as infrastructure, not discovery. A top-venue contribution needs ``we can do $X$
that prior work cannot, here is the falsifiable test that $X$ holds.'' This paper's
$X$ is: \emph{the localization licenses a reusable, training-free repair that
transfers across examples beyond a matched-null repair, and beats
detector-selected localizations fed through the identical repair pipeline}.

\paragraph{The central object: repair-transfer certification.} We define a trace
segment to be \textbf{repair-transfer-certified} for a hallucination class when a
\emph{training-free, source-derived localized repair policy}, applied to held-out
in-class \emph{target} examples, recovers factuality beyond a matched-null repair,
at a bounded edit budget and bounded utility cost --- under the registered
operator, transport map, reference construction, class partition, and matched-null
assumptions (A5--A9, Sec.~\ref{sec:method:assump}). Certification is
\emph{sufficient} evidence of causal usefulness, not a definition of it: a segment
may be causally necessary in place yet not single-span-repairable, and a generic
repair may transfer for non-causal reasons. We therefore drop any ``iff'' framing
and scope the claim to the registered design. The headline statistic is the
cross-example repair-transfer estimand $\Rhat$; the within-example necessity
statistic $\Uhat$ (the matched-null CIU estimand) is demoted to a \emph{screening}
statistic. The patching/repair machinery, idle in a measurement-only design,
becomes the star.

\paragraph{The unifying principle.} One sentence states the whole paper:
\begin{quote}\itshape
Counterfactual usefulness must be earned out-of-sample, under a reference the
method does not control.
\end{quote}
Three surfaces are \emph{instances} of this one principle, not additive tricks:
(i) \textbf{certification} ($\Rhat$, gate G9) earns usefulness out-of-sample
\emph{across examples} (held-out targets); (ii) \textbf{selective-inference
correction} (Sec.~\ref{sec:si}) earns gate verdicts out-of-sample \emph{across the
data-adaptive binning and the operator selection} (both are selection events that
must be paid for); (iii) the \textbf{adversarial oracle} (Axis X$'$,
Sec.~\ref{sec:method:oracle}) earns the reference-validity assumption
out-of-distribution \emph{in the reference} (a regime where the reference is
misspecified and, provably, the controls \emph{cannot} catch it, so $\Rhat$ itself
must collapse). Remove any one and the principle is incompletely tested.

\paragraph{Why $\Rhat$ is not a second $\Uhat$.} $\Uhat$ measures
\emph{necessity in place} (mask the source span in $x_i$, read the factuality drop
in $x_i$): within-example, ablation-style. $\Rhat$ measures \emph{sufficiency
across examples} (apply a source-derived repair policy to a held-out $x_j$, read
the factuality gain in $x_j$): cross-example, repair-style, at a budget, against a
matched-null repair baseline. A necessary-in-place span need not yield a
transferable repair; the two are decorrelated in a scientifically interesting way,
which is exactly why G9 is a \emph{new} falsifiable test and not a restatement of
the necessity gate.

\paragraph{The falsifiable central claim.} Our pre-registered, pre-data claim is:
\begin{quote}\itshape
If hallucination in autoregressive LLMs admits localizable causal segments whose
localization licenses a reusable repair, and the matched-null repair pool is a
valid counterfactual within the stated leakage and reference bounds, then the
cross-example repair-transfer estimand $\Rhat$ clears the repair margin $m_R$
(Holm/selective-inference-corrected two-way-cluster-bootstrap CI lower bound above
$m_R$, class-block-permutation $p<\alpha_1'$) with bounded utility cost
$\Dutilr\le0.02$ and bounded positivity loss on at least two lead datasets, and
the CIU localization out-transfers the best detector-selected localization through
the identical repair pipeline (gate G9-NOV).
\end{quote}
This claim can lose, and each way of losing is routed to a distinct, honest
conclusion (Sec.~\ref{sec:routing}): a transfer failure with a \emph{powered},
theory-informative null routes to a necessity-only paper (gated, not automatic); a
generic-repair pass routes to a diagnostic; a baseline match downgrades novelty;
and a control-silent-yet-certified result under the collinear confounder withdraws
certification and reports a soundness limitation.

\paragraph{Contributions.} Concretely, this paper contributes:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{A certified causal-usefulness estimand} $\Rhat$
  (Eq.~\ref{eq:R}), cross-example and repair-style, with a frozen source$\to$target
  transport map $T$ (Variant~C; Sec.~\ref{sec:method:transport}) carrying a repair
  \emph{policy} rather than a residual state, sitting atop the matched-null CIU
  screening statistic $\Uhat$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:ciu}).
  \item \textbf{An identification lemma with five named assumptions.} A5--A9 make
  every causal premise attackable; Lemma~\ref{lem:rident} splits the assumption-free
  matched-null subtraction from the assumption-loaded causal interpretation
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:assump}, Sec.~\ref{sec:theory}).
  \item \textbf{Valid dependent-pair inference.} $\Rhat$ is a within-class
  U-statistic \cite{hoeffding1948class,lee1990ustatistics}; inference uses a
  two-way (source,target) cluster bootstrap \cite{cameron2011robust,owen2007pigeonhole}
  and a class-block permutation null \cite{good2005permutation}, with a derived
  power model $\sR$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:rvar}) that is \emph{not} the within-example
  $\sU$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:inference}, Sec.~\ref{sec:setup:power}).
  \item \textbf{Selective-inference correction.} Every data-adaptive choice is a
  selection event, paid for by a selection split or an enumerated-grid Bonferroni
  penalty \cite{cox1975note,fithian2014optimal,benjamini2009selective,berk2013validpostselection}
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:si}).
  \item \textbf{An empirical detector contrast, not a rhetorical one.} A baseline
  panel B0--B5 runs detector-selected localizations (including TraceDet
  \cite{tracedet2026diffusion}) through the identical repair pipeline; our novelty is
  \emph{conditional} on out-transferring them (gate G9-NOV; Sec.~\ref{sec:method:baselines},
  Sec.~\ref{sec:setup:gates}).
  \item \textbf{An adversarial oracle with a provable failure regime.} Axis X$'$
  includes a confounder collinear with the leakage controls (which therefore cannot
  trip), where the registered prediction is that $\Rhat$ collapses; if it does not,
  certification is withdrawn for that confounder class (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:oracle}).
  \item \textbf{A complete pre-registered analysis plan with hardened routing}: the
  necessity-only downgrade is publishable only if the transfer null is powered and
  theory-informative (Sec.~\ref{sec:setup}, Sec.~\ref{sec:routing}).
\end{enumerate}

\paragraph{Status.} Following the registered-report methodology
\cite{chambers2014registered,nosek2018preregistration}, this is a Stage-1 design:
method, theory, and a pre-registered, pre-data analysis plan. The CIU estimand
contract and the v4 identification machinery (the graded oracle, the OOD-deflation
correction, the leakage-slope regression, and the nuisance estimators) are
implemented and frozen; the v5 repair-transfer modules (the U-statistic estimator,
the two-way cluster bootstrap, the class-block permutation null, the transport map,
the selection-as-code procedure, and the adversarial Axis X$'$) are \emph{implemented
as do-not-run pure-Python and exercised by a green unit harness (113 passing tests,
no model, no GPU)}. No experiment has been run; the empirical results follow the
authorized run. \texttt{server.authorized} remains \texttt{false} throughout.

\section{Related Work}\label{sec:related}
%==============================================================================

\paragraph{Trace-based detection (TraceDet).} \textbf{TraceDet}
\cite{tracedet2026diffusion} models a diffusion LLM's denoising as an
\emph{action trace} and, via an information-bottleneck objective, isolates the
sub-trace maximally informative about hallucination, training a classifier that
improves detection AUROC by $15.2\%$ on average. This is the sharpest neighbour and
the one whose ``causal beats correlational'' rebuttal must be cashed in
empirically, not asserted. TraceDet is (i) \emph{detection}, optimized for AUROC,
with no intervention estimand: it never edits the trace and reads a factuality
change; (ii) defined on the \emph{diffusion} paradigm. We freeze autoregressive
LLMs as the lead and treat diffusion as a \emph{transfer} study with the identical
operator, so TraceDet enters our AR-lead story in exactly one role: not as a
diffusion detector (that stays in the transfer table) but as a \emph{TraceDet-derived
AR span-selection adapter} --- its IB sub-trace selection criterion re-cast onto
AR-LLM traces --- serving as baseline \textbf{B1} in the repair panel
(Sec.~\ref{sec:method:baselines}), localized to a span and fed through our
\emph{identical} repair pipeline. Our
contribution is \emph{conditional} on out-transferring it (gate G9-NOV): if a
TraceDet-selected span licenses an equally transferable repair, our novelty
downgrades to ``the matched-null + selective-inference certification protocol,''
not ``causal-vs-correlational localization.'' This is the honest, falsifiable form
of the distinction.

\paragraph{Fixed-grid causal tracing and recovery-rate localization (FCCT/IRI).}
Fine-grained Cross-modal Causal Tracing \cite{li2026fcct} adds controlled Gaussian
noise to vision-language inputs, restores clean activations component-by-component
and layer-by-layer, and quantifies the \emph{recovery in output probability} on a
\emph{fixed grid} of components (MHSA/FFN/hidden) and layers; the companion
training-free Intermediate Representation Injection (IRI) reinforces the
strong-effect sites. This is recovery-\emph{rate} tracing: an effect is read off a
pre-enumerated component/layer grid, with no matched-null counterfactual, no
selective-inference correction for the grid choice, and no out-of-sample
\emph{transfer} test --- the localized injection is applied to the same example
whose grid was scored. We differ on all three axes: a per-example \emph{matched}
null (the effect is above-pool, not above-zero), a selective-inference-corrected
treatment of the binning/operator selection, and a \emph{cross-example}
certification (the repair is earned on held-out targets). FCCT/IRI is the
fixed-grid recovery-rate neighbour our net-novelty statement names explicitly.

\paragraph{SFT-based causal mitigation (CDCR-SFT).} \textbf{CDCR-SFT}
\cite{li2026cdcrsft} \emph{trains} an LLM (supervised fine-tuning on a 25{,}368-sample
CausalDR corpus) to construct a variable-level causal DAG and reason over it,
improving causal-reasoning benchmarks and reducing HaluEval hallucination by
$\sim 10\%$. It is a \emph{training-time} mitigation that reshapes the model's
reasoning, with no localization estimand and no per-trace intervention test. We are
\emph{training-free} and \emph{diagnostic}: we localize and certify a span in a
\emph{fixed} model's trace and measure whether editing it transfers, never
fine-tuning. CDCR-SFT operates on variable-level causal structure at the corpus
level; we operate on token-span localization at the trace level. The two are
complementary, not competing, and we position against it to delimit scope, not to
claim a horse-race.

\paragraph{Causal tracing and editing (ROME and the causal-mediation line).}
Mechanistic work locates and edits factual associations
\cite{meng2022rome}, performs causal mediation \cite{vig2020causal}, and studies
causal abstractions and circuits \cite{geiger2021causal,wang2023interpretability}.
ROME-style causal tracing reads an indirect effect of restoring a clean activation
and edits a weight; it is the methodological ancestor of FCCT and of our
\texttt{patch}/\texttt{replay} operators. We borrow the intervention-as-evidence
stance but differ in unit (a check-worthy claim span in a generation trace), outcome
(evaluator factuality elicited under a strictly proper rule), estimand (an
above-pool \emph{matched} contrast, then a cross-example repair-transfer), and
inference (a selective-inference-correct, dependent-pair-valid certification with a
pre-registered kill-gate plan). The novelty is the certified estimand and its
identification design, not any individual operator.

\paragraph{Uncertainty / consistency detectors (the repair-panel baselines).}
\textbf{Semantic entropy} \cite{farquhar2024semantic,kuhn2023semantic} measures
meaning-level output uncertainty; \textbf{SelfCheckGPT}
\cite{manakul2023selfcheckgpt} measures sampling consistency; \textbf{INSIDE}
\cite{chen2024inside} reads internal-state uncertainty; \textbf{RACE}
\cite{race2025reasoning} scores reasoning/answer consistency. All answer an
orthogonal, output- or representation-level predictive question and produce no
intervention estimand. Under our design they are not dismissed: each becomes a
\emph{localization selector} (B2/B3-style) whose span is fed through the identical
repair pipeline, so a strong detector \emph{helps} the baseline beat us --- the
fairness incentive is symmetric, and out-transferring them is the empirical content
of our novelty claim.

\section{The CIU Screening Statistic (preserved core)}\label{sec:ciu}
%==============================================================================

We freeze autoregressive LLM generation as the lead paradigm. A generation produces
tokens $x_{1:T}$ with hidden states $h^{(l)}_{1:T}$ and next-token distributions
$p_t$. The \textbf{primary granularity} is a \emph{claim span} $S=[a,b]$: the
minimal token span an atomic-claim extractor attributes a check-worthy proposition
to. Token windows and reasoning/denoising sub-traces are nuisance/transfer
granularities analysed at a single primary setting to control the confirmatory
family (Sec.~\ref{sec:setup}).

\subsection{The proper-scoring evaluator (mechanism A3, preserved)}\label{sec:ciu:proper}

Let $Y_i\in\{0,1\}$ be a factuality label from a hashed evaluator. To make the
evaluator's probabilistic report $p$ incentive-compatible, it is elicited under the
strictly proper Bregman/Savage binary score
\begin{equation}
S_G(p,y) = G(p) + G'(p)(y-p),
\qquad G_B(p)=1-p+p^2,
\label{eq:savage}
\end{equation}
which reduces (Theorem~\ref{thm:propriety}) to $S(p,y)=1-(p-y)^2$ with unique
maximizer $p=q$ under $Y\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(q)$
\cite{savage1971elicitation,gneiting2007strictly,brier1950verification}. This is
the evaluator pre-commitment assumption A3; it makes no causal claim and is reused
verbatim by the cross-example targets $Y_j$ of $\Rhat$ (with a re-estimated
cross-example agreement $\kappa^{\mathrm{rep}}$, Sec.~\ref{sec:setup:power}).

\subsection{Intervention operators}\label{sec:ciu:ops}

Let $S=[a,b]$ with downstream answer positions $t>b$. The \textbf{primary screening
operator} \texttt{mask} (counterfactual evidence ablation) sets the pre-softmax
attention logits from each $t>b$ to each key $j\in[a,b]$ to $-\infty$ and
renormalizes over surviving keys,
\begin{equation}
\text{for } j\in[a,b]:\ \mathrm{logit}_{t,j}\leftarrow-\infty;
\qquad \alpha_{t,\cdot}\leftarrow\mathrm{softmax}_{\,j\notin[a,b]}\big(q_t\!\cdot\! k_j/\sqrt{d}\big),
\label{eq:mask}
\end{equation}
without editing the residual stream, RoPE/position ids of surviving tokens, or the
logit head; the displaced softmax mass is persisted as \texttt{displaced\_mass}.
The \textbf{repair operators} (the stars of $\Rhat$) are reference-factored:
\texttt{patch} (sufficiency), for $t\in[a,b]$, $l\in L_{\mathrm{patch}}$,
$h^{(l)}_t\leftarrow(1-\alpha)h^{(l)}_t+\alpha\,h^{(l),\mathrm{ref}}_t$;
\texttt{replay}, roll back to the decoder state before $a$, re-decode $[a,b]$ under
a reference policy, free-run the suffix; \texttt{no\_op} is the negative control
($\alpha=0$). \texttt{SHAM-MASK} and an \texttt{answer-adjacent} control isolate the
operator's own footprint and positional/recency effects.

\subsection{The per-example matched null pool $\Pii$ (preserved)}\label{sec:ciu:null}

For operator $\theta$ on $S$ with budget $c(I^\theta_S)=k$, the per-example matched
null pool is
\begin{equation}
\Pii(S) := \mathrm{Uniform}\Big\{\, S'\in x_i :\ c(I^\theta_{S'})=k,\ \mathrm{len}(S')=\mathrm{len}(S),
\ L_{\mathrm{patch}}\ \text{identical},\ S'\cap S=\varnothing,
\ \text{same ref\_hash},\ \text{same } x_i \,\Big\},
\label{eq:pool}
\end{equation}
proximity-stratified to a distance-to-answer bin of width $\dpos$ (holding the
positional-leakage term fixed) and serialized to \texttt{null\_pool\_hash}. Only
\emph{which positions are edited} varies across a draw.

\subsection{The screening estimator $\Uhat$ (demoted from headline)}\label{sec:ciu:estimator}

With $Y_i\in\{0,1\}$ evaluator factuality and $Q_i\in[0,1]$ utility, define under
operator $\theta$ and reference $\rho$
\begin{align}
\delta_i(S;\rho) &= Y_i\!\big(\mathrm{do}(I^{\theta,\rho}_S)\big) - Y_i(\noop),\\
\delta_i(\tilde S;\rho) &= \Eop_{\tilde S\sim\Pii(S)}\!\big[Y_i(\mathrm{do}(I^{\theta,\rho}_{\tilde S}))\big] - Y_i(\noop),\\
u_i(S;\rho) &= \delta_i(S;\rho) - \delta_i(\tilde S;\rho),
\label{eq:ui}
\end{align}
and the CIU estimator $\Uhat(\text{selector};\rho)=\frac1n\sum_i u_i(\Sstar(x_i);\rho)$
with utility drop $\Dutil=\frac1n\sum_i[Q_i(\noop)-Q_i(\mathrm{do}(I^{\theta,\rho}_{\Sstar}))]$.
The OOD-deflated screening statistic $\Udefl$ subtracts a calibrated
\texttt{displaced\_mass} dose-response footprint (Eq.~\ref{eq:deflate}). \textbf{In
v5, $\Udefl$ is a screening gate (G1, preserved), not the headline}: it filters
candidate localizations into the repair-transfer certification, which is where the
causal-usefulness claim is earned.

\subsection{Mask OOD deflation (preserved)}\label{sec:ciu:ood}

On inert spans, any non-zero $\Uhat_{\mathrm{inert}}$ is operator artifact by
construction. Binning \texttt{displaced\_mass} and fitting
$\Uhat_{\mathrm{inert}}=s_{\mathrm{OOD}}\cdot\texttt{displaced\_mass}+c_{\mathrm{OOD}}+\text{noise}$
yields the dose-response slope, whose calibrated footprint at a real span's own
$d^\star$ is subtracted:
\begin{equation}
\Udefl(\Sstar) = \Uhat(\Sstar) - \widehat{\mathrm{art}}\big(\texttt{displaced\_mass}(\Sstar)\big),
\qquad
\widehat{\mathrm{art}}(d^\star)=\hat s_{\mathrm{OOD}}\,d^\star+\hat c_{\mathrm{OOD}},
\label{eq:deflate}
\end{equation}
with the footprint's CI propagated in quadrature.

\section{The Certification Statistic $\Rhat$ (v5 headline)}\label{sec:method}
%==============================================================================

\subsection{The source$\to$target transport protocol $T$}\label{sec:method:transport}

The decisive design choice is \emph{which} object crosses examples. We name three
variants and freeze Variant~C.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Variant A (reference-state transport, rejected).} Inject the
  \emph{source}'s reference residual state $h^{\mathrm{ref}}(x_i)$ at mapped target
  positions. Rejected: cross-example residual states are not comparable (different
  prompt, hidden geometry), positivity fails, and the edit is not a well-defined
  \texttt{patch} on $x_j$.
  \item \textbf{Variant B (target-own reference at mapped positions, rejected as
  headline).} Patch the \emph{target}'s own $h^{\mathrm{ref}}(x_j)$ at positions
  mapped from the source span. Rejected as headline (it reduces to a within-target
  repair whose only cross-example content is the position map, too weak to certify
  transfer); \emph{kept as an ablation} that isolates the position-map contribution.
  \item \textbf{Variant C (source-derived repair \emph{policy} transport, frozen
  headline).} The source localization induces a repair \emph{policy}
  $\policy:=(\mathrm{op},\alpha,L_{\mathrm{patch}},\,\text{budget}\;k,\,\mathrm{ref\_type},\,\text{anchor\_rule})$,
  \emph{not} a state. The policy is applied on the \emph{target}'s own run: the
  target's claim span is identified by the anchor rule (the frozen alignment map
  $T$ below), and the target's \emph{own} reference state is patched/replayed there
  under the source-derived policy. The cross-example content is the policy and
  anchor --- which is exactly what ``the localization licenses a reusable repair''
  means.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{The frozen anchor/alignment map $T$.} $T(\Sstar(x_i),x_j)$ returns the
target span $[a_j,b_j]$ by: (i) restricting to atomic-claim spans of the
\emph{same class} on $x_j$ (taxonomy-matched, frozen partition); (ii) selecting the
in-class span whose proximity bin to the answer matches the source span's
(the same $\dpos$ grid the matched null uses); (iii) matching the edit budget $k$
(length/coordinate count) exactly; (iv) deterministic tie-break (first by position).
If no in-class, budget-matched, proximity-matched span exists on $x_j$, the
\textbf{positivity guard fails} and the pair $(i,j)$ is excluded and recorded (the
A7 positivity event).

\subsection{The estimand and its assumptions}\label{sec:method:assump}

Let $Y_j(\cdot)\in[0,1]$ be the proper-scored evaluator factuality on target $x_j$,
$\noop$ the unedited target run, and $\Pij$ the per-example matched-null repair pool
on $x_j$ (proximity- and budget-stratified, the cross-example analogue of
Eq.~\ref{eq:pool}). Let $\phi_\policy^{[a,b]}$ denote the frozen repair policy
$\policy$ applied at target span $[a,b]$. Define the per-pair \textbf{repair gain}
(Variant~C transport, matched-null-controlled):
\begin{equation}
g_{ij} = \underbrace{\Big[\,Y_j\big(\mathrm{do}(\phi_\policy^{\,T(\Sstar(x_i),x_j)})\big) - Y_j(\noop)\,\Big]}_{\text{localized repair on target}}
\;-\;
\underbrace{\Eop_{S\sim\Pij}\Big[\,Y_j\big(\mathrm{do}(\phi_\policy^{\,S})\big) - Y_j(\noop)\,\Big]}_{\text{matched-null repair on target}}.
\label{eq:gij}
\end{equation}
The \textbf{repair-transfer estimand} is the within-class, source $\ne$ target mean:
\begin{equation}
R(\text{selector};\policy) = \Eop_{(i,j):\,\mathrm{class}(i)=\mathrm{class}(j),\, i\ne j,\, \mathrm{positivity}(i,j)}\big[\,g_{ij}\,\big].
\label{eq:R}
\end{equation}

\begin{assumption}[A5, intervention consistency / SUTVA for the repair]\label{ass:a5}
Applying $\phi_\policy$ at $[a_j,b_j]$ on $x_j$ yields the same $Y_j$ regardless of
which in-class source induced $\policy$, with no cross-target interference (each
target is edited and scored independently)
\cite{rubin1980randomization,imbens2015causal}. \emph{Tested} by the source-swap
diagnostic NC-2 (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:oracle}).
\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[A6, within-class exchangeability]\label{ass:a6}
Targets are exchangeable within a class given the matched-null stratification, so
$\Eop[g_{ij}]$ does not depend on which source induced $\policy$ beyond $\policy$
itself. \emph{Tested} by NC-2 and by class-block permutation
(Sec.~\ref{sec:method:inference}).
\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[A7, positivity]\label{ass:a7}
For a non-vanishing fraction of in-class target pairs an in-class,
proximity-matched, budget-matched target span exists ($T$ is defined). Pairs failing
positivity are excluded and the \emph{excluded fraction is reported}; a class with
excluded fraction $\ge0.5$ is routed to ``insufficient positivity,'' not a null.
\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[A8, reference validity]\label{ass:a8}
The reference run used to build $\policy$ and to patch the target is a valid
counterfactual reference. This is the cross-example analogue of the CIU assumption
A4$^\star$, and the assumption \emph{Axis X$'$ attacks} (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:oracle}).
\end{assumption}
\begin{assumption}[A9, matched-null repair validity]\label{ass:a9}
$\Pij$ is the correct counterfactual repair control: editing \emph{any} matched
in-budget target span the same way is the right ``what if we had repaired a generic
span'' baseline. The $g_{ij}$ contrast subtracts it.
\end{assumption}

\subsection{The G9 baseline panel (the detector contrast made empirical)}\label{sec:method:baselines}

The differentiator is an \emph{empirical margin} over detector-selected
localizations run through the \emph{identical} repair pipeline:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=1pt]
  \item \textbf{B0} --- \texttt{no\_op} floor (target unedited).
  \item \textbf{B1} --- \textbf{TraceDet}-selected \cite{tracedet2026diffusion}
  source span (the IB sub-trace localized to a span), same repair, same transport $T$.
  \item \textbf{B2} --- entropy/uncertainty-selected source span (semantic-entropy /
  perplexity peak), same pipeline.
  \item \textbf{B3} --- probe-selected source span (latent linear probe), same pipeline.
  \item \textbf{B4} --- random same-class source span (the matched-null repair
  baseline of Eq.~\ref{eq:gij}; the control \emph{inside} $\Rhat$).
  \item \textbf{B5} --- oracle-selected source span (planted ground truth, oracle
  fixtures only; upper bound).
  \item \textbf{PROPOSED} --- the CIU selector's $\Sstar$.
\end{itemize}
The headline differentiator is $\Rhat(\text{PROPOSED})-\max_b\{\Rhat(\text{B1}),\Rhat(\text{B2}),\Rhat(\text{B3})\}$,
with its own Holm/SI-corrected CI (gate G9-NOV). If that margin's lower CI does not
clear $0$, the novelty downgrades (Sec.~\ref{sec:routing}).

\subsection{Valid dependent-pair inference}\label{sec:method:inference}

A naive paired bootstrap over $(i,j)$ is invalid because each example recurs as a
source for many targets and a target for many sources: the pairs are
\emph{dependent}. $\Rhat$ is therefore a within-class two-sample U-statistic
\cite{hoeffding1948class,lee1990ustatistics}.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Estimator.} Per class $c$,
  $\Rhat_c=\mathrm{mean}_{(i,j):\,i\ne j,\,\mathrm{pos.\,ok}}\,g_{ij}$; overall
  $\Rhat=\sum_c w_c\Rhat_c$ with pre-registered class weights $w_c$ frozen on the
  selection split.
  \item \textbf{Variance (primary).} A \textbf{two-way (source,target) cluster
  bootstrap} \cite{cameron2011robust,owen2007pigeonhole}: resample source clusters
  and target clusters independently, recompute $\Rhat$ each replicate
  ($\ge10{,}000$); the CI is the percentile interval, and G9 gates on its
  Holm-corrected lower bound.
  \item \textbf{Variance (cross-check).} The ordered-kernel H\'ajek projection variance
  $\mathrm{Var}(\Rhat_c)\approx\zeta_{10}/n_{\mathrm{source}}+\zeta_{01}/n_{\mathrm{target}}$,
  including \textbf{both} the source projection $\Eop[g_{ij}\mid i]$ (variance
  $\zeta_{10}$) and the target-margin projection $\Eop[g_{ij}\mid j]$ (variance
  $\zeta_{01}$), as an analytic bound the cluster bootstrap must not undershoot (the
  symmetric $(4/n_c)\zeta_1$ shorthand understated it and is dropped).
  \item \textbf{Null (corroborating diagnostic, not the confirmatory test).} A
  \textbf{class-block source-block sign-flip} \cite{good2005permutation}: under $H_0$
  ($R=0$), flip an independent $\pm1$ sign on each (class, source) block of the
  matched-null-centred $g_{ij}$ and compare $\Rhat$ to the sign-flip distribution.
  This probes \emph{source-block sign-symmetry / A6 within-class exchangeability} and
  \emph{corroborates} the confirmatory two-way cluster-bootstrap CI; it is \textbf{not}
  exact for $R=0$. Matched-null centring plus A6 deliver mean-zero plus within-class
  exchangeability, which are \emph{strictly weaker} than the distributional
  sign-symmetry a $\pm1$ flip would require (a skewed mean-zero block is exchangeable
  yet not sign-symmetric), so the confirmatory significance burden rests on the
  cluster-bootstrap CI lower bound, with this diagnostic required to agree.
  \item \textbf{Nested matched-null Monte-Carlo uncertainty.} The matched-null term
  in Eq.~\ref{eq:gij} is estimated by $R_{\mathrm{null}}$ draws from $\Pij$; its MC
  variance $\sigma_{\mathrm{MC}}^2/R_{\mathrm{null}}$ is added in quadrature into each
  $g_{ij}$ and propagated through the cluster bootstrap, as is the repair-operator
  stochasticity (e.g.\ \texttt{replay} decoding).
\end{itemize}

\subsection{The repair margin $m_R$}\label{sec:method:margin}

$m_R$ is \emph{not} silently inherited from the necessity margin. It is set to a
design repair margin $m_{R0}$ (a meaningful target-factuality recovery,
\textsc{data\_needed}, pinned on the selection split), attenuation-adjusted by the
cross-example evaluator agreement:
\begin{equation}
m_R := m_{R0}\big/\big(2\,\kappa^{\mathrm{rep}}_{\mathrm{lo}}-1\big).
\label{eq:mR}
\end{equation}
Whether $m_{R0}$ numerically equals the necessity margin's $0.05$ is a selection-split
calibration decision, not an assumption; the locked config records $m_{R0}$,
$\kappa^{\mathrm{rep}}_{\mathrm{lo}}$, and $m_R$. (Formula evaluation only; no value
asserted here.)

\subsection{The falsifiable kill-gates G9 and G9-NOV}\label{sec:method:g9}

\begin{quote}\itshape
\textbf{G9 (repair-transfer usefulness).} On $\ge2$ lead datasets, $\Rhat$
(Eq.~\ref{eq:R}) clears the repair margin with valid dependent-pair inference:
the \emph{confirmatory} Holm/SI-corrected two-way-cluster-bootstrap CI lower bound
$>m_R$, corroborated by the class-block sign-flip \emph{diagnostic} $p<\alpha_1'$;
with bounded utility cost $\Dutilr\le0.02$,
positivity (excluded-pair fraction $<0.5$ per class, A7), and no class/self leakage
(source $\ne$ target). Fail $\Rightarrow$ route per Sec.~\ref{sec:routing}.
\end{quote}
\begin{quote}\itshape
\textbf{G9-NOV (baseline-conditional novelty).}
$\Rhat(\text{PROPOSED})-\max_b\{\Rhat(\text{B1}),\Rhat(\text{B2}),\Rhat(\text{B3})\}>0$
(Holm/SI-corrected lower CI). Fail $\Rightarrow$ the certification protocol is the
contribution, not ``causal beats correlational''; the abstract/Sec.~\ref{sec:related}
novelty downgrades accordingly.
\end{quote}
Like the v4 gates, G9 \emph{never} returns ``invalidated'': it reuses the
fail-closed, no-silent-bypass template. Its routing is honest --- a generic-repair
pass (B4 also clears) is a \textsf{diagnostic}; a utility-trading pass is reframed as
abstention; a baseline match is a novelty downgrade.

\subsection{Adversarial oracle Axis X$'$ (a provable failure regime)}\label{sec:method:oracle}

A clean graded oracle (Axis P/M/D, preserved from the CIU design) can only show
graceful degradation of a \emph{correctly specified} estimator; it can never show
the method detects its \emph{own} misspecification. We add an adversarial axis.
\begin{quote}\itshape
\textbf{Axis X$'$.} The templated generator carries a latent confounder $c_i$ the
reference run does \emph{not} condition on; $c_i$ jointly drives a span's planted
$\tau$ and the selector's covariate (entropy / claim-bearing-ness). The
\texttt{patch}/\texttt{replay} reference is built from the \emph{wrong} $c_i$
(misspecified reference), so \textbf{A8 fails by construction}. Sweep confounding
strength $\xi\in\{0,0.25,0.5,0.75,1\}$ ($\xi{=}0$ reproduces the clean oracle).
\end{quote}
Two sub-regimes, split so the oracle can both \emph{pass} and \emph{fail} its own
prediction:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{X$'$-detectable} ($c_i$ partially orthogonal to the controls). The
  leakage bound (G7) and SHAM/answer-adjacent null (G8) DO move off zero as $\xi$
  rises; the registered prediction is that controls trip \emph{before} $\Rhat$
  certifies a false transferable repair.
  \item \textbf{X$'$-blind} (the \emph{proven} failure regime). $c_i$ is constructed
  to be \emph{collinear with the proximity and displaced-mass covariates}, so by
  construction G7's leakage slope and G8's OOD slope are unchanged --- the confounder
  hides inside the very nuisance the controls estimate, and the controls
  \emph{provably do not trip}. The registered, falsifiable prediction is that
  $\Rhat$ \emph{itself collapses} (the misspecified reference yields a
  non-transferable repair):
  \begin{equation}
  \Rhat(\text{confounded selector};\ \text{misspecified ref})\;\to\;0\ \text{(or negative)}\quad\text{as}\quad\xi\to1.
  \label{eq:p5}
  \end{equation}
  If $\Rhat$ does \emph{not} collapse, the method is unsound for this confounder
  class and the paper says so.
\end{itemize}
A purely correlational score (a detector tracking the confounded covariate) would
still look good while $\Rhat$ collapses --- the cleanest demonstration that
repair-transfer certifies causality where correlation cannot, built to be
\emph{able to fail}. Direct negative controls: \textbf{NC-1} (collinear-confounder)
registers that G7/G8 do not move while $\Rhat$ must collapse; \textbf{NC-2}
(source-swap, tests A5/A6) requires $g_{ij}$ invariant under within-class source
swap up to MC noise, routing a significant source-identity effect to ``re-stratify
the class partition,'' not a silent fix.

\section{Theory}\label{sec:theory}
%==============================================================================

We discharge three results. The proper-scoring identity is a closed-form check
(``formula evaluation, not evidence''); the two identification lemmas (CIU and
repair-transfer) are the load-bearing causal results. Both keep the same
discipline: an \emph{assumption-free} part that sampling alone buys, cleanly split
from an \emph{assumption-loaded} causal part.

\subsection{Propriety for the evaluator (preserved)}

\begin{theorem}[Exact score identity and strict propriety]\label{thm:propriety}
Let $G_B(p)=1-p+p^2$, so $G_B'(p)=2p-1$ and $G_B''(p)=2$. Then the Savage/Bregman
binary score $S(p,y)=G_B(p)+G_B'(p)(y-p)$ satisfies $S(p,y)=1-(p-y)^2$ for
$y\in\{0,1\}$, and under $Y\sim\mathrm{Bernoulli}(q)$ the expected score
$\Eop[S(p,Y)]$ has its unique maximizer at $p=q$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
\emph{(Identity.)} $S=(1-p+p^2)+(2p-1)(y-p)=1-p^2+(2p-1)y$. At $y=0$:
$S=1-p^2=1-(p-0)^2$; at $y=1$: $S=2p-p^2=1-(p-1)^2$; so $S(p,y)=1-(p-y)^2$
identically. \emph{(Strict propriety.)} $\Eop[S(p,Y)]=G_B(p)+G_B'(p)(q-p)$;
the first-order condition $\frac{d}{dp}\Eop[S]=G_B''(p)(q-p)=2(q-p)$ vanishes iff
$p=q$, and the curvature of the expected score is
$\frac{d^2}{dp^2}\Eop[S]=G_B'''(p)(q-p)-G_B''(p)=0-2=-2<0$, so $\Eop[S]$ is
strictly concave with the unique maximum at $p=q$
\cite{savage1971elicitation,gneiting2007strictly}.
\end{proof}
\noindent\emph{(Grid check: $\arg\max_p\Eop[S]=q$ recovered at $q\in\{0.2,0.5,0.8\}$;
numerically estimated curvature $\approx-2$ at all three --- formula evaluation,
not evidence.)}

\subsection{CIU identification (preserved screening lemma)}\label{sec:theory:ciu}

\begin{proposition}[Assumption-free tautology for the CIU pool]\label{prop:tautology}
For each $i$, if $\tilde S_i$ is drawn uniformly from $\Pii(\Sstar)$ and $R$ such
draws are averaged, then $\Eop_{\mathrm{draws}}[\delta_i(\tilde S;\rho)]=\bartau_i(\Pi)$
exactly with Monte-Carlo error $O(1/\sqrt{R})$, where
$\bartau_i(\Pi)=\Eop_{S'\sim\Pii(\Sstar)}[\tau_i(S')]$.
\end{proposition}
\begin{proof}
The expectation of a uniform draw of $\tau_i(S')$ over $S'\in\Pii$ is the pool mean;
no causal or distributional assumption is used, and $O(1/\sqrt{R})$ is the standard
Monte-Carlo rate.
\end{proof}
\begin{assumption}[A4$^\star$: matched pool is the correct counterfactual; testable]\label{ass:a4}
The matched pool $\Pii$ is the correct counterfactual for the selected segment:
editing a budget-matched random segment carries no systematic factuality effect
beyond the position-identity term we net out, with no answer-correlated
positional-leakage term.
\end{assumption}
\begin{lemma}[Unbiasedness of the matched CIU contrast]\label{lem:unbiased}
Under (A1$'$) matching of operator/budget/length/layers/reference/example, (A2) a
shared no-op, (A3) a proper-scored evaluator (Theorem~\ref{thm:propriety}), and
(A4$^\star$),
$\Eop[\Uhat(\text{selector})]=\Eop_i[\tau_i(\Sstar)]-\Eop_i[\bartau_i(\Pi)]$.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
The shared no-op cancels in each $u_i$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:ui}); A3 makes both arms
expectations of a fixed incentive-compatible function; Prop.~\ref{prop:tautology}
gives the random arm without bias; the targeted arm estimates $\tau_i(\Sstar)$;
average over $i$. A4$^\star$ enters \emph{only} in interpreting $\bartau_i(\Pi)$ as
the correct counterfactual.
\end{proof}
\noindent The lemma gives an unbiased estimator of an above-pool contrast; it does
\emph{not} entail detectors must score $0$ (a novelty question), and it is the
\emph{screening} guarantee on which the repair-transfer certification is built.

\subsection{Repair-transfer identification (the headline lemma)}\label{sec:theory:rident}

\begin{lemma}[Identification of the repair-transfer estimand]\label{lem:rident}
Fix the frozen policy $\policy$, transport $T$, class partition, and matched-null
repair pool $\Pij$. Then:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em,itemsep=2pt,label=(\alph*)]
  \item \emph{(Assumption-free part.)} By construction of $\Pij$ and the paired
  $\noop$ subtraction,
  \begin{equation}
  \Eop[g_{ij}] = \big(\text{target-repair effect of the transported policy}\big)
              - \big(\text{mean target-repair effect of a matched-null policy}\big),
  \label{eq:rident-free}
  \end{equation}
  which holds \emph{definitionally} for the registered $\Pij$, exactly as
  Lemma~\ref{lem:unbiased} holds for $\Uhat$. No causal claim is used here.
  \item \emph{(Assumption-loaded part.)} Under A5--A9
  (Sec.~\ref{sec:method:assump}), $R(\text{selector};\policy)$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:R})
  equals the average controlled repair-transfer effect attributable to the
  \emph{localized} policy rather than to generic repair or to confounding. Hence
  $R>0$ is interpreted as causal usefulness \emph{only} under A5--A9, never
  definitionally.
\end{enumerate}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
\emph{(a)} The inner expectation in Eq.~\ref{eq:gij} is, by definition of $\Pij$,
the mean repair effect of a uniformly-drawn matched-null target span, and the first
bracket is the repair effect of the transported localized span; the shared $\noop$
cancels in both, giving Eq.~\ref{eq:rident-free} as an identity (Monte-Carlo error
$O(1/\sqrt{R_{\mathrm{null}}})$ on the inner term).
\emph{(b)} A9 makes the subtracted term the correct generic-repair counterfactual;
A5 (SUTVA-for-repair) makes each target's edited outcome well-defined and
source-independent given $\policy$; A6 (within-class exchangeability) makes
$\Eop[g_{ij}]$ depend on the source only through $\policy$, so the within-class mean
over source $\ne$ target pairs estimates a single policy-attributable effect; A7
(positivity) makes the conditioning event in Eq.~\ref{eq:R} non-degenerate; A8
(reference validity) makes the repair a valid counterfactual rather than an
OOD artifact. Under A5--A9 the residual after the A9 subtraction is the controlled
effect of the \emph{localized} policy, identifying $R$ as the average controlled
repair-transfer effect.
\end{proof}
\noindent\textbf{What the lemma gives and does not give.} \emph{Gives:} a
selective-inference-correct, dependent-pair-valid certification that a positive,
CI-clearing $\Rhat$ is the above-matched-null transfer effect of the localized
policy, under A5--A9. \emph{Does not give:} that a detector-selected span cannot
also transfer (the empirical content of G9-NOV), nor robustness to a confounder
collinear with the controls (the explicit X$'$-blind failure mode), nor necessity
(that is the screening $\Uhat$). The split mirrors the CIU tautology/A4$^\star$
scaffold: each clause is attackable, and Axis X$'$ stress-tests A8.

\subsection{Positional-leakage sensitivity (preserved)}\label{sec:theory:leakage}

\begin{lemma}[Positional-leakage bound]\label{lem:leakage}
Let $\beta$ be the maximum per-position leakage slope and $\dpos$ the maximum
proximity gap $\Pii$ allows. Then the worst-case A4$^\star$ bias is bounded:
$|\Eop[\Uhat]-U_{\mathrm{true}}|\le\beta\,\dpos$. Proximity-stratifying $\Pii$ drives
$\dpos\to(\text{bin width})$; the answer-adjacent control estimates $\beta$ by
$\delta_{\mathrm{inert},i}=\beta\,\mathrm{proximity}_i+\gamma+\varepsilon_i$ on
validation only, and the gate uses the upper-CI product
$B_{\mathrm{UCI}}:=\beta_{\mathrm{hi}}\,\dpos$.
\end{lemma}
\noindent Gating on $\beta_{\mathrm{hi}}$ keeps the positive-A4$^\star$ gate
conservative; the same logic gates the X$'$-detectable prediction (controls trip
before $\Rhat$ certifies).

\section{Selective Inference}\label{sec:si}
%==============================================================================

\textbf{Every data-adaptive choice is a selection event that must be paid for}
\cite{benjamini2009selective,berk2013validpostselection,fithian2014optimal}. v5
corrects two layers a measurement-only design leaves open (the proximity/displaced-mass
binning) and one v5 adds (the repair-operator selection).

\paragraph{SI-1 --- selection split (primary).} Partition validation into a
selection split $V_{\mathrm{sel}}$ and an inference split $V_{\mathrm{inf}}$, both
disjoint from test \cite{cox1975note}. \emph{All} data-adaptive choices --- the
proximity-bin width $\dpos$, the \texttt{displaced\_mass} edges, the repair policy
$\policy$ (the operator freeze OS-1), the class weights $w_c$, and $m_{R0}$ --- are
frozen using $V_{\mathrm{sel}}$ only. Nuisance estimates and all gate statistics are
computed on $V_{\mathrm{inf}}$/test with every selection held fixed, so the Holm
family on $V_{\mathrm{inf}}$/test is valid \emph{without} post-selection adjustment:
$K_{\mathrm{bin}}=K_{\mathrm{op}}=1$ for the inference family.

\paragraph{SI-2 --- selection-conditioned Bonferroni (fallback).} If splitting costs
too much power (the $n_{\mathrm{val}}$ floors bind on both splits), pre-register a
multiplicity penalty over the reachable, enumerated selection grid: the binning
ladder gives $K_{\mathrm{bin}}$, the operator grid (op $\times\,\alpha\times
L_{\mathrm{patch}}\times\,\mathrm{ref\_type}$) gives $K_{\mathrm{op}}$, both bounded
before data. The confirmatory level is
\begin{equation}
\alpha_1' = \frac{0.05}{m'\cdot K_{\mathrm{bin}}\cdot K_{\mathrm{op}}},
\label{eq:sialpha}
\end{equation}
folded into the Holm family. The deterministic choice rule (SI-1 iff the
$n_{\mathrm{val}}$ floors are met on both splits, else SI-2) is frozen, so the power
cost is known before lock.

\paragraph{Selection-as-code.} The deliverable is a frozen, unit-tested
\texttt{select\_binning($V_{\mathrm{sel}}$)} $\to$ (binning, selection\_event) and an
operator freeze on $V_{\mathrm{sel}}$, each emitting the chosen value \emph{and} the
selection event, so the SI correction is computable from the recorded event, not
narrated.

\subsection{Sampling levels and the confirmatory family}\label{sec:setup:family}

We separate sampling levels with distinct symbols: examples (the cluster margins of
the two-way bootstrap), pairs $N_{\mathrm{pair}}$ (the U-statistic kernel
evaluations, \emph{not} independent), seeds (floor $20$; reported as seed-variance),
matched-null repair draws $R_{\mathrm{null}}$ (controlling the inner
$O(1/\sqrt{R_{\mathrm{null}}})$ MC error of Lemma~\ref{lem:rident}a, default
$\ge16$), repair-operator repeats $R_{\mathrm{int}}$ (default $\ge8$), and bootstrap
resamples $B_{\mathrm{boot}}$ (default $\ge10{,}000$ \cite{efron1994bootstrap}). \textbf{Primary confirmatory cells (pre-registered, feasible).} The \emph{primary}
confirmatory point is the two-cell set
\texttt{Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct} $\times$ \{\textbf{TriviaQA}, \textbf{HotpotQA}\} ---
one model family, two datasets. This 2-cell point is what the Holm confirmatory
family below is sized for and what the headline $\Rhat$/G9/G9-NOV claim is
adjudicated on. \textbf{Secondary (budget-gated).} The full 4-cell grid that
\emph{adds} \texttt{Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct} on the same two datasets is
\emph{secondary}: it is run only if the recomputed forward budget clears the
ceiling (Sec.~\ref{sec:setup:feasible} / \texttt{redesign\_v5\_ar\_lead.yaml}
\texttt{decision\_order}), and it tests cross-family generalization, not the
primary claim. The
Holm confirmatory family \cite{holm1979simple} comprises, at the locked primary
settings, the preserved gates G1, G2, G7, G8, and the v5 gates G9 (per lead cell)
and G9-NOV (per B1--B3 contrast) across the primary $\{\texttt{Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct}\}
\times \{2\ \text{datasets}\}$ point; the second model size enters only the secondary,
budget-gated grid. Operator and granularity are nuisance dimensions at a single
primary setting. Under SI-1 ($K_{\mathrm{bin}}=K_{\mathrm{op}}=1$) the realized
family size $m'$ and per-test $\alpha_1'=0.05/m'$ are recorded in the locked config;
under SI-2, $\alpha_1'$ also pays $K_{\mathrm{bin}}K_{\mathrm{op}}$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:sialpha}).

\subsection{Leakage-safe nuisance estimation and the $\sR$ power model}\label{sec:setup:power}

Nuisance parameters that set the power budget are estimated \emph{only} on
$V_{\mathrm{sel}}$/$V_{\mathrm{inf}}$, with named estimators, CIs, minimum
$n_{\mathrm{val}}$, and a freeze-at-lock rule; the test split stays sealed.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{$\sU$ (screening).} Sample sd of the within-example paired contrasts
  $u_i$ (hard bound $\sU\le0.707$); power uses the upper CI via $\ge10{,}000$
  resamples; minimum $n_{\mathrm{val}}\ge200$ paired examples per cell.
  \item \textbf{$\kappa$, $\kappa^{\mathrm{rep}}$ (attenuation).} Cohen/Fleiss
  agreement \cite{cohen1960coefficient,fleiss1971measuring} on the binary factuality
  label, double-scored, for the within-example ($\kappa$) and cross-example
  ($\kappa^{\mathrm{rep}}$) targets; the attenuation correction uses the lower CI
  (Spearman tradition \cite{spearman1904attenuation}). Minimum $n_{\mathrm{val}}\ge300$
  double-scored items per cell; a degenerate bootstrap blocks the lock.
  \item \textbf{$\sR$ (certification power model --- DERIVED, not borrowed).} $\sU$ is
  the wrong scale for a cross-example U-statistic. We derive
  \begin{equation}
  \mathrm{Var}(\Rhat)\;\approx\;\frac{\zeta_{10}}{n_{\mathrm{source}}}+\frac{\zeta_{01}}{n_{\mathrm{target}}}
  \;+\;\frac{1}{N_{\mathrm{pair}}}\Big(\frac{\sigma_{\mathrm{MC}}^2}{R_{\mathrm{null}}}+\frac{\sigma_{\mathrm{op}}^2}{R_{\mathrm{int}}}\Big),
  \label{eq:rvar}
  \end{equation}
  where the kernel $g_{ij}$ is \emph{ordered/asymmetric} (source role $\ne$ target
  role), so the first-order projection has \textbf{two} parts: $\zeta_{10}$, the
  variance of the \emph{source} projection $\Eop[g_{ij}\mid i]$, divided by the source
  cluster count $n_{\mathrm{source}}$; and $\zeta_{01}$, the variance of the
  \emph{target-margin} projection $\Eop[g_{ij}\mid j]$, divided by $n_{\mathrm{target}}$.
  Both projections are retained (the symmetric shorthand $(4/n_{\mathrm{eff}})\zeta_1$
  collapsed the two margins and understated the asymmetric-kernel variance; it is no
  longer used). $\sigma_{\mathrm{MC}}^2/R_{\mathrm{null}}$ is the nested matched-null MC
  variance and $\sigma_{\mathrm{op}}^2/R_{\mathrm{int}}$ the repair-operator
  stochasticity. Class imbalance enters through $n_{\mathrm{source}}/n_{\mathrm{target}}$
  and a pre-registered design-effect $D_{\mathrm{eff}}\ge1$; $n_{\mathrm{eff}}=
  \min(n_{\mathrm{source}},n_{\mathrm{target}})$ is retained for design-effect
  bookkeeping only.
\end{itemize}
The certification power count is
\begin{equation}
R_{\mathrm{power}} = \Big\lceil\big(z_{1-\alpha_1'/2}\,\sigma_{R,\mathrm{hi}}/m_R\big)^2\,D_{\mathrm{eff}}\Big\rceil,
\label{eq:rpower}
\end{equation}
with $\sigma_{R,\mathrm{hi}}$ the upper CI of $\sR$ on $V_{\mathrm{sel}}$, computed in
a NEW estimator (the v4 necessity power count is \emph{not} reused). All of
$\zeta_{10}$, $\zeta_{01}$, $\sigma_{\mathrm{MC}}$, $\sigma_{\mathrm{op}}$,
$D_{\mathrm{eff}}$, $n_{\mathrm{source}}$, $n_{\mathrm{target}}$ are
\textsc{data\_needed}, estimated at lock; Eq.~\ref{eq:rvar}/\ref{eq:rpower} are the
frozen identities the locked config evaluates.

\subsection{Exhibited feasible operating point (re-checked, not assumed)}\label{sec:setup:feasible}

The v4 necessity design exhibited one feasible

\section{Stage-2 Decision Routing}\label{sec:routing}
%==============================================================================

Each way the central claim can lose routes to a distinct, honest conclusion. The
necessity-only downgrade is \emph{conditional and powered}, not a default escape
hatch.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{R1 (full certification).} G9 + G9-NOV + the Axis X$'$ predictions
  pass $\Rightarrow$ the headline causal repair-transfer claim, scoped by A5--A9.
  \item \textbf{R2 (necessity + protocol).} G9 fails but the certification
  \emph{protocol} (matched-null + selective inference) is itself a validated
  contribution and B1--B3 lose $\Rightarrow$ report the protocol as the contribution
  (weaker but honest).
  \item \textbf{R3 (necessity-only downgrade --- GATED).} Report the screening-level
  necessity paper \emph{only if} the G9 null is (i) \emph{powered}
  ($n\ge R_{\mathrm{power}}$ of Eq.~\ref{eq:rpower}, so the null is informative, not
  under-powered) \emph{and} (ii) \emph{theory-informative} (a pre-registered
  mechanism prediction, e.g.\ ``single-span training-free repairs do not transfer for
  class $c$ because the error is distributed,'' tied to the multi-cause Axis M). A
  null that is under-powered or mechanism-silent is \emph{not} publishable as a
  finding; it is ``inconclusive --- insufficient power,'' routed to a budget request.
  \item \textbf{R4 (unsound).} The X$'$-blind prediction fails (controls silent,
  $\Rhat$ stays certified under the collinear confounder) $\Rightarrow$ certification
  is \emph{withdrawn}; report the soundness limitation. No causal-usefulness claim.
\end{itemize}

\section{Limitations}\label{sec:limitations}
%==============================================================================

We carry the design's caveats forward honestly.
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em,itemsep=2pt]
  \item \textbf{Repair-transfer may be the wrong difficulty.} $\Rhat$ could be
  $\approx0$ for most classes if single-span training-free repairs rarely transfer.
  This is \emph{not} auto-publishable as ``still a complete paper''; it is
  publishable only via R3 --- a \emph{powered} ($n\ge R_{\mathrm{power}}$) and
  \emph{theory-informative} (pre-registered mechanism) null --- else it is
  ``inconclusive, request budget.''
  \item \textbf{Construct validity: certification is sufficient, not necessary --- and
  neither may be reliable.} A segment may be causally necessary in place yet not
  single-span-repairable, and a generic repair may transfer for non-causal reasons; we
  drop ``iff'' and scope the claim to the registered design (A5--A9). A skeptical
  reviewer can press further: the chosen operationalization (``repair-transfer $=$
  causal usefulness'') is neither necessary nor reliably sufficient for the construct,
  so even a clean R1 (full certification) demonstrates a narrow, operator-/transport-/
  class-partition-dependent phenomenon, not the general claim a title might imply. We
  accept this scoping explicitly; the screening $\Uhat$ captures necessity that $\Rhat$
  may miss, and we do not claim the construct beyond the registered operators.
  \item \textbf{The detector contrast is empirical and can lose.} If a
  detector-selected span (B1--B3) out-transfers PROPOSED through the identical
  pipeline (G9-NOV fails), the contribution shrinks to ``the matched-null +
  selective-inference certification protocol'' --- a real but lateral,
  infrastructure-grade contribution rather than ``causal beats correlational.'' This is
  an acceptance risk we accept and state up front, not after the fact.
  \item \textbf{The confirmatory test does not borrow validity from A6.} The G9
  significance burden is carried by the two-way cluster-bootstrap CI, which assumes
  neither a sharp null nor source-block sign-symmetry. The class-block sign-flip is a
  \emph{corroborating diagnostic} for A6 within-class exchangeability, not the
  confirmatory test: matched-null centring plus A6 give mean-zero plus exchangeability,
  which are strictly weaker than the distributional sign-symmetry a sign flip would
  need, so we do \emph{not} claim it is exact for $R=0$ (the earlier
  ``exact-by-construction under A6'' assertion is withdrawn). The primary $p$-value
  therefore does not borrow validity from the very A6 premise it helps probe.
  \item \textbf{A8 (reference validity) is the one substantive cross-example
  assumption, and it is non-identifiable on real AR-LLM traces.} Axis X$'$-blind is,
  to our knowledge, the first oracle in this line that \emph{provably can fail} (a
  confounder collinear with the controls); the paper claims only ``$\Rhat$ collapses
  on the planted X$'$-detectable and X$'$-blind confounder classes,'' explicitly
  \emph{not} ``robust to all confounding.'' We state the sharper concession plainly:
  the X$'$-blind regime discharges A8 only on a \emph{synthetic} generator whose
  $\Rhat$ collapse ($\text{transferred}=\tau_0(1-\xi)$) and whose control silence are
  \emph{hand-coded identities}, so this ``provable failure regime'' is a correctness
  fixture (a unit test promoted to a falsification), \emph{not} an adjudication of the
  real-data causal question. On real AR-LLM traces A8 remains non-identifiable; the
  oracle bounds the \emph{method's} behaviour under a known confounder, it does not
  certify A8 holds in the wild. This is the single substantive premise a skeptical
  reviewer may decline to credit, and we do not claim the oracle removes it.
  \item \textbf{The headline causal claim is unfalsifiable at Stage-1 (no data yet).}
  Every empirical slot is \textsc{data\_needed} and \texttt{server.authorized} is
  \texttt{false}, so this submission asks acceptance of the \emph{protocol} and its
  closed-form identities over synthetic fixtures, not of a certified causal result. A
  top venue may reasonably decline to credit the causal \emph{headline} (as opposed to
  the certification protocol) before any data exists. We state this as an accepted
  acceptance risk, not a resolved one: what is falsifiable \emph{now} is the machinery
  (the green do-not-run harness); the causal claim is falsifiable only after the
  authorized run.
  \item \textbf{Positivity may be low, and the anchor $T$ is matched on the covariates
  the estimand nets out (self-defined-ruler risk).} A7 is measured and reported;
  classes with excluded-pair fraction $\ge0.5$ are routed to ``insufficient
  positivity,'' not counted as a null. The anchor map $T$ is locked to the same
  $\dpos$ proximity grid and exact budget $k$ that define the matched-null pool
  $\Pij$, so PROPOSED and its B4 control are matched precisely on the structural
  covariates $\Rhat$ subtracts. This (a) tightens the ``self-defined ruler'' critique
  --- the contrast is sharp but narrow --- and (b) risks low positivity, an empirical
  unknown that could route many classes to ``insufficient positivity'' and silently
  shrink the certifiable population, capping power and external validity. We report A7
  per class rather than assuming it, but the certifiable population size is genuinely
  unknown pre-data.
  \item \textbf{U-statistic inference is heavier and the SI correction is
  conservative.} The two-way cluster bootstrap is $O(B\cdot N_{\mathrm{pair}})$ pure
  CPU on already-collected $g_{ij}$ with a closed-form H\'ajek cross-check; the
  SI-1/SI-2 path is chosen by a deterministic rule known before lock, so the power
  cost is known, not discovered. $\sR$ is \textsc{data\_needed}, not assumed.
  \item \textbf{The graded oracle is synthetic.} It demonstrates correct degradation
  on a templated generator with planted ground truth; it does not show that real AR
  traces share the same $\tau$ structure. It is a correctness fixture (no paper
  numbers).
  \item \textbf{Repair forwards raise compute.} Folded into Eq.~\ref{eq:rpower}'s
  forward surcharge and the v4 decision order; the locked config records it.
  \item \textbf{Scope and evidence.} A CI-clearing $\Rhat$ is consistent with the
  existence of localizations whose repair transfers within a class, never ``all
  hallucinations are causally explained.'' Replay is expensive, so this is offline
  causal \emph{auditing}, not a live decoder. \textbf{No empirical evidence exists in
  this submission}: the only numerics are the closed-form / quadrature checks,
  explicitly labelled ``formula evaluation, not evidence,'' and
  \texttt{server.authorized} remains \texttt{false}.
\end{itemize}

%==============================================================================
\section{Conclusion}\label{sec:conclusion}
%==============================================================================

We have replaced ``honest measurement of a self-defined contrast'' with a
\emph{certification}: a trace segment is repair-transfer-certified for a
hallucination class when a training-free, source-derived localized repair
\emph{policy} recovers held-out in-class targets beyond a matched-null repair. The
cross-example statistic $\Rhat$ is the headline; the within-example matched-null
necessity statistic $\Uhat$ is the screening filter beneath it. We name the
source$\to$target transport map and the five assumptions (A5--A9) under which $\Rhat$
is an identified causal estimand, prove an identification lemma that separates the
assumption-free matched-null subtraction from the assumption-loaded causal
interpretation, and make the inference \emph{valid for dependent pairs} (a within-class
U-statistic with a confirmatory two-way cluster bootstrap CI, corroborated by a
class-block sign-flip diagnostic) and the verdicts \emph{selective-inference correct}
(every data-adaptive choice is a paid-for selection event). The detector contrast is empirical, not rhetorical: a baseline panel
runs detector-selected localizations through the identical repair pipeline, and our
novelty is conditional on out-transferring them. An adversarial oracle includes a
\emph{provable} control-failure regime where $\Rhat$ must collapse or the method is
declared unsound. The full analysis plan, hardened routing, and an exhibited,
re-checked feasible operating point are pre-registered; the preserved CIU machinery is
implemented and frozen, and the v5 repair-transfer machinery is likewise
\emph{implemented as do-not-run pure-Python}, covered by the green unit harness
(113 passing tests, no model, no GPU). Empirical validation follows the
pre-registered, authorized run; until then \texttt{server.authorized} remains
\texttt{false}, no run is executed, and every result is a \textsc{data\_needed}
placeholder.
